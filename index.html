The Case

Five points, plainly put.

Set down in order, the way I would lay it out across the table. Read them straight through.

i.

I am not sales

Sales people make an intro and walk away. I brought the client, built the site, managed the relationship, and handled support. That is not a commission situation.

ii.

The foundation was not finished

He says the platform poured the foundation, framed it, wired it, and handed over a finished working business. It had gaps. I waited on his changes to do basic things and filed bug reports while I built. The fixes were features BE1st needed anyway, that any future developer benefits from. A foundation you repair after building starts was never finished, and that is beta testing, not a service to me.

iii.

I carry all the risk

One hundred percent of the client risk lands on me. If the client is unhappy, wants revisions, or the relationship goes sideways, that is mine to handle. He has zero client exposure.

iv.

The house does not sell itself

A finished house with no buyer is worth zero. I found the buyer, walked them through the door, and closed the deal. Without me there is no revenue to divide at all. The platform alone generated zero dollars.

v.

His own analogy sinks it

Realtors keep three to six percent of the sale and the builder keeps the rest. If I am the realtor in his story, then the platform keeps three to six, not seventy. He flipped every role to make it work, and it still did not hold.

You are the general contractor. The platform is a vendor you use. Vendors do not take the majority cut.
